Do and don't list on mobile

Previous research

No previous research found.

User need

As a user I NEED to easily understand what I should and should not do in my situation SO THAT I have the best outcome possible.

Design and research

Notes

The maximum line length in the current design was 37 characters. The recommended optimal line length is between 45 and 75 characters.

Previous lab sessions showed that users could miss information on the right side of the page.

The dos and don'ts are not comparative lists.

First iteration

Hypothesis

If we stack the lists on desktop
Then users will find it easier to read
Because they will be left aligned and be within the optimal line length.

Research findings

2 x Pop-ups, 4 x Usability labs

  • Users really liked them, made lots of comments about how useful they are.
  • Users saw and understood both the do and don'ts.
  • They saw the green ticks as a positive "do" action and the red as a warning.

Accessibility

People with a visual disability may not be able to see the ticks and crosses. For this, we have prefixed the don't list items with the word 'don't'.

Testing this with users who use screenreaders, proved that this was the right thing to do.

It was unnecessary to prefix the do list items with the word 'do', as it was annoying users.

Usage

Keep your points as brief as possible.

Dos normally come before Don'ts.

It is alright to just have Don'ts if there aren't any Dos.

Future developments

To make the component more versatile, we are planning to make the heading editable There is a ticket for this in the redesign backlog.
